Roller Skiing

What is it?

Much like regular Skis where you push and slide with your legs, Roller Skis are meant to be used on dry surfaces instead of snow. Therefore, roller Skis simulate the same effect as regular Skis and even use the same Ski boots.

Why is its purpose?

Many Olympic competitions require Skis, such as Freestyle Skiing, Biathlon, Cross-Country Skiing and a couple of others. Many athletes who participate in these competitions must practise year-round, even when it's summer; how do you think they do that? They use the power of Roller Skis! There's even a saying in Scandinavian countries that Skiing is a summer sport for in winter you should be competing and in the summer you should be practising.

Classical Style:

With this style, the Skis are always parallel to the driver, and so are the sticks. The right arm and the left foot are synchronized with each other, and so are the left arm and the right foot. On classical style roller Skies, the wheels are thicker than skate style, as it helps with balance.



Skate Style:

With this style, the Skis operate much like regular skates; this method can be faster, where the right foot and right arm are synchronized, vice versa. On skate-style roller Skis, the wheels are thinner, as less width is needed to maintain balance.
